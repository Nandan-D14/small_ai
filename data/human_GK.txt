User: Could you explain how deep learning works?

AI: Deep learning models use artificial neural networks to learn patterns in data. These networks are composed of layers of nodes, or "neurons," which mimic the way biological neurons work in the human brain. Each layer in the network processes data and passes it on to the next layer. The first layer might learn basic features like edges in an image, while deeper layers might learn more complex patterns, such as objects or faces. The final layer produces the output, like a classification label for an image or a predicted word in a sentence. During training, the model adjusts the weights of connections between nodes to minimize error and improve its predictions.
